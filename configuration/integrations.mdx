## Slack

The Slack integration sends formatted event notifications to any channel in your workspace.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Integrations">
    In your Google dashboard, navigate to **Settings → Integrations** and click **Connect** next to Slack.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize the app">
    You are redirected to Slack's authorization page. Select the workspace and channel where you want to receive notifications, then click **Allow**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose events">
    Back in the Google dashboard, select which events should appear in Slack (e.g., account created, webhook failure, rate limit warning).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test the connection">
    Click **Send Test Message** to confirm a test notification appears in the selected channel.
  </Step>
</Steps>

To disconnect, click **Disconnect** on the Slack integration card. Google will immediately stop sending notifications to Slack.

## Zapier

The Zapier integration exposes Google events as Zapier triggers, letting you connect them to over 6,000 apps.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the Google app">
    Log in to [zapier.com](https://zapier.com) and search for **Google** in the app directory.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a Zap">
    Click **Create Zap**, choose **Google** as the trigger app, and select an event type (e.g., **Account Created**).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your account">
    When prompted, enter your Google API key to authorize the Zapier integration.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the action">
    Choose the action app (e.g., send an email, create a row in Google Sheets) and map the trigger data to the action fields.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Activate the Zap">
    Turn on the Zap. Google will now send events to Zapier every time the trigger fires.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Custom integrations

For any integration not covered by the built-in connectors, use the [REST API](/api-reference/overview) or [webhooks](/guides/webhooks) directly. Webhooks deliver real-time POST payloads to any HTTPS endpoint you control, giving you full flexibility to integrate with internal systems, custom workflows, or any HTTP-capable service.
